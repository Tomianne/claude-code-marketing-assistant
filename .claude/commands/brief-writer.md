Turn rough campaign thinking into a structured creative brief.

## Step 1: Load context
Read context/brand-voice.md, context/target-audience.md, and context/campaigns.md.

## Step 2: Gather missing information
Only ask if genuinely unclear -- do not ask for things that can be reasonably inferred:
- What is the campaign trying to achieve? (awareness, leads, retention, launch)
- Who specifically is it targeting?
- Which channels will it run on?
- Is there a deadline or budget to factor in?

## Step 3: Write the brief

Structure:
```
# Campaign Brief: [Campaign Name]
Date: YYYY-MM-DD

## 1. Objective
[One sentence. Include a metric if possible.]

## 2. Target audience
[Specific, not generic. Reference target-audience.md and add any campaign-specific focus.]

## 3. Key message
[The single idea this campaign communicates. One sentence.]

## 4. Tone of voice
[Reference brand-voice.md plus any campaign-specific tone notes.]

## 5. Channels and formats
[List each channel and what gets produced for it.]

## 6. Deliverables
[Bullet list of every asset that needs to be produced.]

## 7. Timeline
[Brief approved / assets delivered / campaign live / campaign ends.]

## 8. Success metrics
[2-3 specific measures.]
```

## Step 4: Save and present
Save to projects/briefs/YYYY-MM-DD-[campaign-slug].md
Present in full. Ask: "Does this capture it, or anything to adjust?"
